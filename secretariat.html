Secretary-General

Samrat Pratap Singh
Secretary-General

A delegate, debater, and self-claimed orator, he’s either drafting geopolitically impossible yet oddly creative communiqués or scribbling smut you shouldn’t read. Often found in kitchens—hunting food or cooking it—his culinary flair fuels one belief: he will be a great man (for her, name withheld). An ex-cricketer for Eastern Railways and state swimming champion, his fall came from two things: rising pressure on the ground and his greatest love—politics. With blunt questions and high expectations, your best bet is to run if you find him in committee. Today, he serves as Secretary-General of SMUN.

Deputy Secretary-General

Shadan Husham
Deputy Secretary-General

People call him Saddam Hussein. He doesn’t correct it, it’s funny, and he respects the man as a role model for decisiveness and chaos management. Runs on coffee, caffeine, and broken sleep cycles. Late nights are spent awake for no reason other than to make everyone around him miserable. Weak arguments? Crushed. Drafts? Torn to shreds. Anime keeps him twitchy and jittery. Research gives him ideas that are extreme, reckless, and probably illegal. Political opinions? Absolute chaos. No one sane can follow them, and that’s exactly the point. Mistakes stick to him like flies. Sanity avoids him. Patience doesn’t exist in his vocabulary. He doesn’t lead, just guides things from the sidelines. He’s the Deputy Secretary-General of SMUN—a caffeinated, chaotic, probably destructive force.
